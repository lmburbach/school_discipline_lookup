School Discipline in the State of Georgia

The State of Georgia’s student discipline data is collected, aggregated at the school level, and made publicly available by the Governor’s Office of Student Achievement. According to this data set, discipline incident refers to a student behavorial infraction for which the school issued one of the following consequences: alternative school, corporal punishment, detention, expulsion, in-school suspension, juvenile court/court referral, out-of-school suspension, physical restraint, removed from class, or suspension from bus. Such incidents are categorized as attendance/dress code, cheating, drugs/alcohol, gang-related behavior, harm to person, harm to property, repeated offenses, sexual offenses, or weapons.

The tool below summarizes school discipline data from the 2016-2017 (the most recent year available) for a selected school or school system and shows trends since the 2013-2014 school year. By selecting different subgroup categories, you can explore the difference in enrollment population and disciplined popualtion for various subgroup categories: race/ethnicity, gender, disability status, economic status, and English learner status.

In addition to the discipline data from the Governor's Office of Student Achievement, additional data on student enrollment by race was gathered from the Urban Institute's Education Data Explorer tool.
